# FishSlayR — Angler's Guide

Log your catches, build a fishing journal, and run tournaments with the crew. It works on the water even with **no signal** — everything saves to your phone first and syncs when you're back in range.

---

## Getting started

1. Open **https://bboard01.github.io/FishSlayRBeta/** on your phone.
2. Sign in with Google.
3. **Add it to your home screen** so it opens like a real app and works offline:
   - **iPhone:** Share → *Add to Home Screen*
   - **Android:** menu (⋮) → *Install app* / *Add to Home Screen*

Once installed, you don't need signal. Log all day; it syncs itself when the bars come back.

---

## The basics: trips and catches

Everything you log hangs on a **trip**. Start a trip when you hit the water, log fish to it, end it when you're done.

**To log a fish:**
1. Start a trip (name it, pick the water — takes 10 seconds).
2. Tap **Land a Fish**.
3. Pick species → set length → snap a photo → add details if you want (lure, color, depth — all optional).
4. Save.

That's the whole loop. The fish lands in your Livewell and your journal.

## The pages

The app is a set of screens, reachable from the side rail (desktop) or the bottom bar (phone).

### 🛶 Boathouse — home base
Your starting screen. Shows how the day's going, your active trip, and a big **Land a Fish** button. Start here.

### 🐟 Livewell — your wall of fish
Every fish from a trip, shown as cards. Filter and sort them, see your trip stats and the day's pattern. **Tap any fish to open or edit it** (fix the length, add a photo, add notes). You can also flip back through past trips.

### 📖 Journal — your fishing story
Your seasons shelved by year, each trip written up as a "story cover." Open any trip to relive it. Tap **🎣 Spin a Fish Tale** on a trip to have the app write a short story of your day.

### 🧠 River Intelligence — what's working
Read-only "what catches fish for me" view. Filter by water, species, or method and it breaks down your best lures, colors, depths, and conditions. Use it to plan the next trip.

### 🌊 Waters — your places
A passport of the waters you fish. Each spot gets its own page with a map of your catches, your history there, and the species you've pulled out of it.

### 🪝 Tackle Box — your rigs
Save your go-to setups as **Quick Rigs** so you can drop them onto a catch fast instead of re-typing lure/color/etc. every time.

### 🏆 Legends — your trophy room
Your personal bests and milestones. Best fish by species, biggest ever, and achievement plaques. "Not records. Legends."

### 🎣 Tournament — compete with the crew
Host or join tournaments, log tournament fish, and watch the leaderboard. Full details below.

### ⚙ Rig Box — settings
Manage your **seasons**, switch between **Dock and Water mode**, sound settings, back up your data, and the reference lists. Your settings sync across your devices.

---

## Seasons

A **season** is a chapter of your fishing year — all your trips live inside one. Most anglers just fish the season that's already active and never think about it. When you want a fresh start (new year, new goals), start a new season in the **Journal** or **Rig Box**. Your stats, journal, and intelligence all follow the active season.

## Tournaments

### Joining one
1. Get the **join code** from the host.
2. **Tournament** page → enter the code.
3. Pick your team (or start a new one).

Done — you'll see the leaderboard and your team's standing.

### Hosting one
1. **Tournament** → **Host**.
2. Name it, set start/end times.
3. **Make sure the window covers when you'll actually fish** — fish caught outside it don't count.
4. Share the join code.

### Logging a tournament fish
On the water, switch to **Water Mode** for the big-button view.
1. Tap **🎣 LAND A FISH**.
2. Species → length → **take a photo**.
3. Land it.

It goes straight to the board and your rank updates.

> **Bass need a photo to count.** No photo = no score. This is the one thing to remember.

### Dock Mode vs. Water Mode
- **Dock Mode** — the full dashboard: leaderboard on one side, your tournament livewell on the other. Use it at the ramp or between spots.
- **Water Mode** — stripped down, big buttons, for logging fast while fishing. Toggle **🌊 Water Mode** from the dashboard; tap **↩ Dock** to go back.

### Your tournament livewell
Your fish for *this* tournament, kept separate from your regular journal so your personal log stays clean.
- In **Dock Mode** it's beside the leaderboard. In **Water Mode** tap **🐟 Livewell**.
- Shows your count, bass, top-3 inches, and biggest.
- **Tap any fish to open or edit it.**

**The red ⚠ "to fix" flag:** a fish with a red flag didn't count — almost always a bass with no photo. The flag tells you why. **Tap it → add the photo → done.** It scores on the next refresh.

A **⏳ "not on the board yet"** note just means you're offline or haven't picked a team. It'll publish once you're back online.

---

## Tips

- **Always photo your bass.** Can't say it enough.
- **Pull to refresh** the board to see teammates' latest fish.
- **Offline is fine.** Log everything; it syncs when signal returns.
- **← All tournaments vs. red Leave:** the back arrow keeps you in the tournament (just leaves the view). The red **Leave** button actually drops you out — don't hit it by accident.
